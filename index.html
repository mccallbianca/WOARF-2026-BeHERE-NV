The Problem BeHERE NV Was Built to Solve

Nevada's Workforce Crisis
Is Structural. So Is Our Solution.

Assembly Bill 37 gave BeHERE NV a mandate. ECQO-Care gives that mandate the infrastructure to fulfill it — not as a vendor, but as a co-architect of Nevada's first AI-integrated behavioral health workforce pipeline.

01 / THE LABOR TRAP

Graduate Programs Cannot Close the Gap Alone

Nevada's behavioral health workforce deficit is structural — not a staffing shortage in the traditional sense. Graduate pipelines are capped by faculty capacity, supervision limits, and economics. Meanwhile, demand accelerates. No recruitment strategy alone can solve a mathematics problem.

02 / BURNOUT + RETENTION

Recruitment Without Retention Is a Revolving Door

The providers BeHERE NV successfully recruits are leaving faster than the pipeline replaces them — driven by high-acuity caseloads, professional isolation, and wages that cannot sustain a career. ECQO-Care absorbs Tier 1 volume, protecting human capacity for the work only humans can do.

03 / THE DATA VOID

Underrepresented Communities Are Missing From the Research

Black, Indigenous, Latino, LGBTQIA+, rural, and neurodivergent populations are systematically excluded from the research that designs interventions for them. ECQO's Virtual Human effect — proven to increase disclosure for stigmatized experiences — generates the culturally valid data Nevada's workforce strategy has never had.

04 / NO CAREER LADDER

Peers and CHWs Have No Technology-Integrated Pathway Forward

Peer support specialists and Community Health Workers — the populations BeHERE NV most aggressively recruits — face lower wages, fewer advancement pathways, and no credential that positions them in the emerging AI-integrated behavioral health economy. ECQO-Care builds that ladder.

How This Partnership Directly
Addresses WOARF Priority 3.

Priority 3 calls for multidisciplinary, culturally appropriate overdose prevention and education — with workforce capacity as a core sustainability requirement. ECQO-Care and BeHERE NV together answer every dimension.

3

Multidisciplinary OUD Prevention + Workforce Education

ECQO deploys clinically governed AI as 24/7 prevention infrastructure — delivering OUD psychoeducation, existential risk screening, and overdose prevention content at scale to Washoe County adults. BeHERE NV's workforce pipeline ensures that the peer specialists, CHWs, and lived experience workers who govern the human layer of this system are recruited, trained, and certified through a rigorous NSHE-aligned curriculum.

Culturally Appropriate Outreach for Under-Resourced Communities

BeHERE NV's existing relationships with Black, Indigenous, Latino, LGBTQIA+, and rural Nevada communities — and Dr. Miranda-Alfonzo's 20+ years of BIPOC-centered recruitment and outreach — ensure that ECQO-Care's peer cohort reflects and resonates with the populations it serves. ↳

Workforce Sustainability + Staff Burnout Mitigation

WOARF explicitly prioritizes programs that address provider shortages and burnout. ECQO absorbs 90% of Tier 1 prevention volume, freeing licensed clinicians for complex clinical work. BeHERE NV's retention infrastructure — combined with the ECQO Circular Economy model that generates supplemental income for certified professionals — creates the economic and professional conditions that keep the workforce in the field.

↳

Sustainability Beyond the Settlement Window

The ECQO-ARM, ECQO-AM, and ECQO-ANS certification tracks are designed for NSHE integration, legislative recognition, and connection to the ECQO Circular Economy — where $0.75 PMPM from all Nevada platform users is recycled into peer workforce infrastructure statewide. This program does not require perpetual grant renewal to survive. Note: Desert Rose Gives supports workforce pathway expansion and does not operate as a Medicaid provider. BeHERE NV's legislative relationships position this partnership to seek formal state appropriation at the next session.

↳

Rural Health Transformation Grant Alignment (WRRAP + RHIT)

Initiative 3 (WRRAP) prioritizes CHWs and peer support specialists as scalable rural workforce solutions — exactly the ARM and AM certification targets. Initiative 4 (RHIT) funds consumer-facing AI and digital health infrastructure that meets CMS Health Technology Ecosystem standards — ECQO's architectural mandate. BeHERE NV's NSHE anchor and Dr. Hartzell's rural infrastructure expertise make this partnership the RHT proposal CMS designed the program to fund.

Implementation Roadmap

Three Years From Pilot
to Permanent State Infrastructure.

UNR claims first position as the NSHE pilot campus. BeHERE NV carries the model to the full Nevada System — and to the Legislature — from there.

P1
Phase 1 · April – June 2026 · Formalization

MOU Execution + Institutional Alignment

Execute Memorandum of Understanding between ECQO Holdings, Desert Rose Gives, and BeHERE NV. Define IP framework, data governance protocols, and shared evaluation methodology. Dr. Manit initiates NSHE elective credit alignment review. Dr. Pruitt begins policy pathway mapping. BeHERE NV identifies NSHE pilot campus priorities beyond UNR.

P2
Phase 2 · July – December 2026 · Pilot Launch

ARM Cohort Launch + Evaluation Baseline

First cohort of 20 ARM candidates recruited from BeHERE NV pipeline — priority given to peer support specialists and CHWs in rural and underserved Washoe County communities. 16-hour curriculum delivered on NSHE pilot campus. 90-day incubator begins with co-supervision from UNR CASAT and BeHERE NV. Dr. Lovitt establishes baseline workforce data: burnout indicators, satisfaction metrics, intention-to-remain measures.

P3
Phase 3 · 2027 · Evidence Build + NSHE Expansion

Evaluation Publication + Board of Regents Presentation

Pilot outcomes published in partnership with BeHERE NV data team. Findings presented to NSHE Board of Regents as evidence base for formal ARM credit recognition. AM and ANS cohorts launch across additional NSHE campuses. Joint RHT grant application submitted during appropriate federal cycle. Dr. Hunt leads legislative preparation for next session.

P4
Phase 4 · 2028–2029 · Legislative + Statewide Scale

State Recognition + Permanent Workforce Infrastructure

BeHERE NV and ECQO Holdings present to the Nevada Legislature for formal state recognition and appropriation of ECQO certification as a permanent behavioral health workforce pathway. Full NSHE statewide deployment — from Southern Nevada to rural and frontier campuses. Nevada moves from 51st in mental health access to first in AI-integrated behavioral health workforce innovation.

Grant Objectives
The WOARF grant funds Desert Rose Gives to deploy the ECQO conversational AI platform as 24/7 behavioral health prevention infrastructure for Washoe County residents — addressing OUD and co-occurring behavioral addictions through a multidisciplinary, peer-governed, tech-enabled model. Over three years, the program trains and certifies peer specialists from lived-experience and CHW cohorts in AI-integrated roles (ECQO-ARM, ECQO-AM, ECQO-ANS); delivers real-time existential screening, overdose prevention education, and warm-handoff pathways; and builds an evidence base to scale ECQO certification into the Nevada System of Higher Education and toward state legislative recognition and federal sustainability funding.
ECQO-Care Program Goals
Goal 1 — Prevention Infrastructure: Deploy ECQO as clinically governed, 24/7 digital prevention infrastructure absorbing Tier 1 volume and protecting the human workforce for high-acuity clinical work.

Goal 2 — Workforce Development: Train and certify peer specialists and CHWs in three technology-integrated roles (ECQO-ARM, ECQO-AM, ECQO-ANS) — Nevada's first AI behavioral health workforce credentials — housed inside NSHE through BeHERE NV partnership.

Goal 3 — Circular Economy + Legislative Permanence: Activate ECQO's $0.75 PMPM workforce reinvestment mechanism and pursue formal state legislative recognition of ECQO certification at the next Nevada legislative session, creating a permanent, self-sustaining behavioral health workforce pathway.
